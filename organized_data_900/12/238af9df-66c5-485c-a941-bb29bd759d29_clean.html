    Procedure Details
    Risks and benefits of the surgery were discussed in clinic, and any remaining question were answered in the preoperative area. She was marked in the preoperative area. Pt was then taken to the operating room and placed in a supine position on the operating table. After successful induction of anesthesia, pt was padded and secured to the operative table.  Methylene blue diluted 1:9 was then injected at the edge of the areola. Pt was prepped and draped in the standard fashion.

     
    Left SLNB began through a 5 cm transverse axillary incision. This was deepened with cautery through the subcutaneous fat to access the axillary nodal pocket.  The C-Trak probe was used to direct this dissection, going towards the highest radiation signal.  In the process I look for any blue coloration. Any palpable or blue nodes were removed and sent to pathology, and and the nodes with the highest signal on Ctrak were sent to pathology until the background was less than 10% of the highest. The pocket was irrigated and hemostasis was confirmed. I then turned to complete the mastectomy.

     
    Then I shifted to the incision planned for the mastectomy, using a double S configuration around the nipple areola complex.  The incision was deepened with electrocautery, and skin flaps were raised in all directions to separate the breast tissue from the skin flap.  Care was taken to ensure even thickness of skin flaps, which for this patient was quite thin as she had very little subcutaneous fat.  The mastectomy pocket became continuous with the axillary dissection pocket at the lateral margin of the breast. The breast tissue was then elevated off of the underlying pec muscle fascia to complete the excision. It was marked with suture for orientation before being sent to pathology. A single 15 French channel drain was then placed coming out the inferior lateral aspect of the dissection. Only one drain was required as the axillary and breast pockets were continuous.  The drain was connected to bulb suction.  Skin was then closed at the deep dermal layer with 3-0 Vicryl at both incisions, and then skin was then closed with running Monocryl.  This was covered with Steri-Strips and Mastisol followed by gauze and tegederm.

     

    Compressive dressing was then constructed using fluffs, ABD pads, and an elastic postoperative bra. 

     
    Patient tolerated the procedure well.  All counts were reported as correct.  Patient awoke from anesthesia without incident and was transferred to the PACU in good condition.
