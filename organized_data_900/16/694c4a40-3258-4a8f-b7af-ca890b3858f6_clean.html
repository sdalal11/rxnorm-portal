      - Replacement of saturated fat with dietary mono- and poly-unsaturated fats can be beneficial

      Exercise and Physical Activity
      To reduce Cardiovascular Disease risk, adults should:
      - Be routinely counseled to optimize a physically active lifestyle

      - Engage in at least 150 minutes per week of accumulated moderate intensity or 75 minutes per week of vigorous intensity aerobic physical activity (or an equivalent combination of moderate and vigorous activity). This includes adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus

      - Decrease sedentary behavior

      For adults unable to meet the minimum physical activity recommendations:
      - Engaging in some moderate or vigorous intensity physical activity, even if less than this recommended amount, can be beneficial to reduce Cardiovascular Disease risk
