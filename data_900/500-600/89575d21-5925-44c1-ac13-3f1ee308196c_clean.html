PROCEDURE IN DETAIL: The patient was brought to the operating room, underwent LMA anesthesia, placed in supine position, prepped and draped in the usual sterile fashion. Received preoperative antibiotics and sequential compression boots placed to lower extremities. Prior to induction of anesthesia, time-out was performed. A left hemiscrotal incision was made in a horizontal fashion. The different layers of the scrotum were opened. The hydrocele was then delivered through the scrotal wound. It was quite tense and large. Upon delivering the hydrocele, it was dissected out completely. The hydrocele was then opened. Dark old blood was drained. No way testicle was identifiable. It appeared that the testicle had become necrotic and atrophied. At one point, it was unclear if this could be a scrotal hernia, but general surgeon was called to evaluate. The intraoperative consult was done. They inspected, did an exam intraoperatively and confirmed that it was not a scrotal hernia. No communication with the peritoneal cavity. The hydrocele sac was then opened up completely and excised. There was no identifiable testicle in this area. Once completed, most of the sac was removed. The area was irrigated copiously and hemostasis was achieved. The dead space was closed with 3-0 chromic suture material in interrupted fashion. Skin was closed with 4-0 chromic suture material in interrupted fashion. A 0.25% Marcaine plain, 10 cc injected. The patient was extubated. Scrotal support and fluffs were placed. The patient extubated and sent to the recovery room in stable fashion. Follow up in my office [REDACTED].
