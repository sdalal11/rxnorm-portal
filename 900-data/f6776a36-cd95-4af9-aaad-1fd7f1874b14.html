    DISCUSSION:
    We spoke about behavior modifications (reducing caffeine, alcohol, spicy food etc), decreasing fluid at nights. Medical therapy may help. Alpha blockers work by relaxing the smooth muscles in the bladder and prostate. Improvements in symptoms are expected within a few days but can take 1 to 3 months. Common SE include dizziness, fatigue, congestion, orthostatic hypotension and retrograde ejaculation.

    5aRIs work by inhibiting conversion of T to DHT, thus decreasing prostate volume by ~25%. 5aRI therapy can reduce the risk of retention, surgery, and PSA by 50%. But it usually takes several if not 6-9 months to feel symptomatic improvement. Improvements are normally seen in those with >40cc glands. SE include impotence (<5%), decreased libido (<5%), decrease ejaculate volume, and gynecomastia (<1%). This is especially useful in men with gland greater than 30 cc.

    There have been also been studies that have shown a benefit of tadalafil for men with both ED and BPH. Tadalafil has improved symptom scores, but has not shown a measurable improvement in urine flow rate.

    We also discussed surgical options for BPH, including TURP, greenlight laser vaporization, and UroLift to name a few. But we discussed it is useful to undergo a trial of medication prior to proceeding with these. If a surgical intervention is being considered, I discussed that it would be important to undergo a diagnostic cystoscopy for urethral measurement and evaluation of median lobe.
