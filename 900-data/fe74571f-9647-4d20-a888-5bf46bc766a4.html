Patient education provide on insomnia which includes but is not limited to, Change your lifestyle to

promote good sleep Avoid caffeine, especially in the afternoon or evening. Coffee, cola and tea are obvious sources of

caffeine. Caffeine may also hide in chocolate, medicines, diet pills and soft drinks. Avoid alcohol before bedtime. Alcohol

may help you fall asleep, but it will make you wake up again later on in the night. Avoid tobacco and any other stimulating

drugs at bedtime. Ask your doctor if any of the medicines you take may be causing sleep problems. Exercise regularly.

However, avoid exercising within three or four hours before you go to bed. Try eating a light snack before bedtime, such as

crackers and milk. But don't eat a heavy meal close to bedtime. Improve your sleep environment, Keep the temperature in

your bedroom cool, but not cold. Keep the bedroom dark and quiet. Be sure your mattress is comfortable. Slow down your

mind at bedtime, Many people find it hard to turn off their mind at bedtime. If this happens to you, gently remind yourself

that you can think about these things tomorrow. Then set the thoughts aside. You may have to try this several times before

your mind quiets down. Setting up a special time early in the evening to worry and think may keep you from doing it at

bedtime.
